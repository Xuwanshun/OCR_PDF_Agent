The IRS Form 1040 instructions provide guidance on using the Tax Table to figure your tax. Specifically, it mentions that you should refer to the Tax Table to determine your tax amount based on your taxable income and filing status. For example, if a married couple filing jointly has a taxable income of $25,300, they would find the corresponding line in the Tax Table and read down the column for their filing status to find the tax amount, which in this case is $2,562. Additionally, there are instructions indicating that if your taxable income is less than $100,000, you should use the Tax Table, while amounts of $100,000 or more require the use of the Tax Computation Worksheet.
